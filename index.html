Urchade Zaratiana

I am a PhD student at Laboratoire Informatique de Paris Nord (LIPN), under the supervision of Nadi Tomeh and Thierry Charnois. My research focuses on structured prediction for Natural Language Processing.

I am interested in the science of deep learning in general, including topics such as domain generalization, zero/few-shot learning, instruction tuning, learning under noisy data/labels, scaling laws, and intriguing phenomenons such as the 'double descent', the 'grokking mechanism' and the 'simplicity bias'.

During my PhD, I have worked on the following research problems:

  • Language model pretraining
    Training a BERT-like model without positional encoding (ICLR 2024 Tiny Paper)
  • Constrained decoding of language model (2022-)
    Autoregressive text to graph model for Information extraction: preliminary version published at ICML 2023 workshop (Link), and final version at AAAI 2024 (Link)
  • Detection of machine-generated text
    Our system ranked top 2 at SemEval "Multidomain, Multimodel and Multilingual Machine-Generated Text Detection" shared task using 300M parameters model. Top 1 used a 70b model. (Link)
  • Efficient and exact decoding for information extraction
    • Named Entity Recognition as Structured Span Prediction (Link)
    • EnriCO: Constrained decoding of information extraction using logical rules. (Link)
  • Graph (structure) learning
    • GraphER: End-to-end graph structure learning of joint entity and relation extraction (Link)
    • GNNer: using graph (and Graph Neural Network) to implicitly constrain the output of neural network (Link)
  • Improved training algorithm (loss functions)
    • Filtered Semi-CRF (Link)
    • Global Span Selection (Link)
  • Arabic Information extraction
    • Cross-Dialectal Named Entity Recognition in Arabic (Link)
    • A Span-Based Approach for Flat and Nested Arabic Named Entity Recognition: Top 1 system at Wojood NER shared task (Link)
